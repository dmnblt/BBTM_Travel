History

Tourism in Kazakhstan is a branch of the country's economy, revenues from which in 2014 amounted to 0.3% of its gross domestic product.

By 2020, the government of Kazakhstan plans to bring this figure to 3%. In 2000, 1.47 million foreign tourists visited Kazakhstan. By 2012, their number had risen to 4.81 million, placing the country 51st among all countries in terms of the number of visits for tourism purposes.

In the world press, the country's tourism industry is described as underdeveloped, despite impressive natural attractions; Poor service and logistics difficulties are cited as factors hindering the flow of tourists.

In 2013, the Kazakh government developed a concept for the development of the tourism industry of the Republic of Kazakhstan until 2020. The goal of the program is to create five so-called tourist clusters in the state: Nur-Sultan, Almaty, East Kazakhstan, South Kazakhstan and West Kazakhstan. The concept provides for attracting investments in the amount of $ 4 billion and creating more than 250 thousand jobs in the tourism industry by 2020
